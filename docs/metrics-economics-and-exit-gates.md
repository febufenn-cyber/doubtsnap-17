# Metrics, Economics, and Exit Gates

## Unit of value

A resolved doubt requires correct reconstruction, a valid and verified method, disclosed uncertainty, and evidence that the student is less confused or can transfer the concept.

## Metric hierarchy

### North-star candidate

Verified doubts resolved per active student per week.

### Quality

- critical-field transcription accuracy
- canonical-method validity
- final-answer and unit accuracy
- high-confidence error rate
- solver/verifier disagreement rate
- unsupported-assumption rate
- student still-confused rate
- transfer-question correctness
- correction-request rate

### Product

- time to first useful intervention
- reconstructed-question confirmation rate
- guided-step participation rate
- learning-loop completion rate
- second-doubt rate
- 7-day and 30-day return rate
- check-my-work adoption

### Business

- cost per attempted doubt
- cost per verified doubt
- cost per resolved doubt
- free-to-paid conversion
- resolved doubts per paying user
- paid retention
- support and refund reasons

## Error severity

- S0 Cosmetic: wording or formatting only
- S1 Teaching weakness: unnecessarily long, too revealing, or weak transfer check
- S2 Recoverable misunderstanding: non-critical extraction issue or suboptimal valid method
- S3 Incorrect result: wrong principle, arithmetic, value, sign, or unit
- S4 Critical: invented critical data, confident false teaching, privacy breach, or cross-user access

Recurring S4 model failures or any authorization S4 block expansion.

## Cost envelope

The original price hypothesis is ₹149 per month. Phase 0 must model payment fees, taxes, infrastructure, support, storage, and AI usage before final packaging.

Track cost independently for:

- image quality and safety check
- transcription and structuring
- classification
- canonical solving
- verification
- tutoring transformation
- follow-up generation and verification

Optimization principles:

- reject unreadable input before expensive reasoning
- compress and crop safely
- route easy tasks to lower-cost models
- escalate only when uncertainty or complexity justifies it
- cache exact or canonical textbook matches without leaking user data
- meter by successfully handled doubt rather than raw request count

Provisional target: preserve a gross margin compatible with 30–60 successfully handled doubts per typical paid user. This must be replaced with measured provider costs.

## Phase 0 evidence gate

Required before Phase 1 implementation is accepted:

- 15–20 observed student sessions
- at least 5 teacher or tutor interviews
- at least 5 parent interviews if parents remain a buyer hypothesis
- at least 100 verified benchmark items
- at least 20 ambiguous, degraded, or adversarial cases
- generic-model baseline on at least 30 items
- three language styles tested
- comparison of full-solution, guide-me, and check-my-work interaction
- measured cost range for easy, medium, and hard problems
- documented provider retention and privacy constraints

## Go criteria

- a narrow group repeatedly presents the target problem
- the diagnostic workflow improves transfer or reduces confusion
- critical transcription and solution errors can be detected rather than hidden
- the opening wedge has reachable distribution
- cost per resolved doubt supports a viable allowance
- users return with another real doubt

## Pivot triggers

- students value check-my-work but reject snap-and-explain
- tuition teachers are the only reliable acquisition channel
- regional language is not important but curriculum specificity is
- broad vision input is unreliable while typed or cropped input works
- consumer pricing fails but the engine creates institutional value

## Stop criteria

- generic multimodal chat performs equivalently with no durable workflow advantage
- students overwhelmingly seek answer-only behavior and do not engage with learning
- repeated high-confidence errors remain undetectable
- privacy requirements cannot be satisfied within the intended flow
- cost cannot support pricing or a credible higher-value buyer
- real-user access is insufficient to validate the product
