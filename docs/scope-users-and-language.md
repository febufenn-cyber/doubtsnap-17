# Scope, Users, and Language

## Provisional opening wedge

- Curriculum: Tamil Nadu State Board
- Grade: Class 11
- Subject: Physics
- Initial domain: mechanics
- Input: one printed or clearly handwritten problem, optionally with student working
- Question language: English initially
- Explanation styles under test: English, Tamil, Tamil-English

The wedge remains provisional until Phase 0 evidence supports it.

## Primary user

A Class 11 student who recognizes the chapter or formula family but gets stuck translating a word problem into a diagram, selecting the governing principle, applying signs and units, or correcting an attempted solution. They currently rely on tuition teachers, friends, WhatsApp, YouTube, guidebooks, search, or generic AI.

## User states

### Cannot understand the question

Required behavior: rewrite simply, list known and unknown quantities, explain key language, and avoid solving immediately.

### Does not know which principle applies

Required behavior: identify the concept, contrast plausible methods, and ask the student to choose or reason.

### Started but got stuck

Required behavior: inspect the work, locate the first invalid step, and intervene there.

### Got a different answer

Required behavior: compare paths and identify the divergence in assumptions, signs, units, algebra, or arithmetic.

### Wants a fast result

Required behavior: provide a concise verified path while keeping reasoning visible and offering an optional check.

## Buyer hypotheses

- Student subscription: fast personal help and revision continuity
- Parent purchase: evidence of learning rather than copying
- Tuition-centre distribution: fewer repetitive doubts and aggregate misconception insight

Phase 0 must identify which buyer can be reached and trusted without expanding the initial product into three separate products.

## Initially supported

- one problem per image
- clear printed or legible handwritten text
- short conceptual and numerical mechanics questions
- standard school-level notation
- diagrams with visible labels
- student attempts with traceable steps
- units, vectors, graphs, and equations represented in the supported schema

## Initially unsupported or clarification-only

- entire examination papers or bulk worksheets
- unreadable, heavily cropped, or glare-obscured inputs
- advanced olympiad or calculus-based physics
- long formal derivations
- laboratory reports requiring unavailable observations
- questions requiring external tables not shown
- chemistry, biology, and general mathematics
- unsupported regional curricula until separately evaluated
- questions with insufficient information for a unique answer

Unsupported input must be identified honestly rather than forced through the solver.

## Language experiment

Test three explanation styles on the same concepts:

1. English: syllabus-consistent terminology
2. Tamil: simple conceptual Tamil with carefully validated scientific terms
3. Tamil-English: conversational explanation while preserving formal equations and exam terminology

Measure comprehension, reading speed, comfort, perceived credibility, transfer success, and preference. Do not assume full translation is automatically better.

## Core jobs to be done

Functional: help the student get unstuck and solve a related problem.

Emotional: provide patient, private help without judgment or wasted time.

Social: help the student participate confidently in class or tuition without exposing their confusion publicly.
